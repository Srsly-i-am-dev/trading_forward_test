Fundamentals/Context: This setup identifies market contraction (consolidation) followed by expansion (breakout). Big players create a tight range to build positions without alerting other traders and to create liquidity via stop losses above and below the range.

Setup:

Identify an Institutional Candle: A larger candle that sets the initial range (High/Low).
Wait for 8 to 10+ Inside Bars: Smaller candles forming entirely within the High and Low of the Institutional Candle.
Entry Logic:

Buy: Enter when price breaks above the high of the channel.
Sell: Enter when price breaks below the low of the channel.
Note: The signal is stronger if it occurs near a support or demand level.
Exit Logic (Management):

Stop Loss: Place below the low of the contraction phase for a buy, or above the high for a sell.
Take Profit: Target a move equal to the size of the move before the contraction phase, or at minor support/resistance levels.

Stop Loss: Just outside the wick of the pin bar.

Fundamentals/Context: Used when no clear Institutional Candle is present. It relies on a rejection candle at a key level followed by a consolidation pause.

Setup:

Identify a Pin Bar (wick is at least 50% of total range) at a support/resistance or demand/supply level.
Wait for an Inside Bar to form immediately after, indicating a pause in volatility.
Entry Logic:

Buy: Enter on the break of the High of the Inside Bar, in the direction of the Pin Bar.
Sell: Enter on the break of the Low of the Inside Bar, in the direction of the Pin Bar.
Exit Logic (Management):

Stop Loss: Below the low of the pin bar for a buy, or above the high for a sell.
Take Profit: At the next opposing support or resistance level.
